Image Of The Day

Rare Blue Supermoon 8/19/24

The Sturgeon Moon rises behind a replica Saturn V
rocket at the U.S. Space & Rocket Center in
Huntsville, Alabama on Monday, August 19, 2024.
Over 99% full when it rose, the moon was a rare
combination of a blue moon and a supermoon, a
phenomenon that will not repeat until 2027. Image
credit: NASA/Michael DeMocker

Earth
Information
Center

For more than 50 years, NASA satellites have provided data on Earth's land,
water, air, temperature, and climate. NASA's Earth Information Center allows
visitors to see how our planet is changing in six key areas: sea level rise and
coastal impacts, health and air quality, wildfires, greenhouse gases,
sustainable energy, and agriculture.
